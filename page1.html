What is a Computer Network?

A computer network is a collection of interconnected devices that can communicate and share resources with each other.

What is a Social Network?

A social network is a structure that describes the relationships between individuals or organizations, often facilitated by online platforms for communication and interaction.

ARPANET

(Advanced Research Projects Agency Network) was the basis of the Internet. It was established in 1969 by the US Department of Defence.

Glossary

An entity in a social network (sn) with example
People, groups, or organisations with profiles in a social network. They post content, interact, form relationships, and participate in events. For example, a person with a Facebook profile who posts updates and connects with friends.
Bandwidth
Network bandwidth is a measurement indicating the maximum capacity of a wired or wireless communications link to transmit data over a network connection in a given amount of time.
A relationship in a sn with example
Connections between entities based on interactions, shared interests, communication, or exchanges. Represented as edges in a network graph. For example, two friends who follow each other on Instagram and comment on each other's posts.
Binary/Decimal
Binary: A string of 0s and 1s, which is the input from the computer, translated by the computer into its own understanding. Decimal: A base‑10 number system using digits 0–9, the standard system humans use for counting.
An event in a social network with example
An event in a social network could be a gathering or online activity where entities participate. For example, a virtual meetup on Zoom where participants share ideas and collaborate.
Bits, Bytes, Kilobytes, Megabytes
Bits, Bytes, KiloBytes, Megabytes all are a specific amount of storage used by an application, or a document. Megabyte is 1024 KB, and KB is 1024 Bytes.
Sociogram
A visual map of relationships within a group, showing who interacts with whom and highlighting popularity, isolation, or subgroups.
Data vs Information
Data: Information sent across a network, broken into packets containing bits, destination addresses, error checking, and sometimes encryption for secure transmission. Information: Processed, organized, and structured data that provides context and meaning for decision-making.
Six Degrees of Separation
The theory that everyone is connected to everyone else through a chain of no more than six acquaintances.
Internet of Me/ Internet of Things
Internet of Me (IoMe): The Internet of Me (IoMe) loosely refers to technology which connects our minds and bodies with the online world. It transforms our biological and cognitive life into streams of data which can be monitored, shared and shaped. Internet of Things (IoT): Internet of Things (IoT) encompasses smart devices and smart objects that send and receive information using the internet and communication infrastructure.
Digital footprint
The trail of data you leave online, including posts, searches, uploads, and interactions that can be traced back to you.
Cloud Computing
A mode of computer data storage in which digital data is stored on servers in off-site locations. The servers are maintained by a third-party provider who is responsible for hosting, managing and securing data stored on its infrastructure.
Digital citizenship
Using technology safely, responsibly and respectfully, understanding online rights, responsibilities, and appropriate behaviour.
