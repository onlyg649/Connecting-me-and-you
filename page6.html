Threats to Network Security

Types of malware and viruses e.g. trojan
Malware: Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorised access to computer systems. Trojans: Malware disguised as legitimate software; once opened, it gives attackers access or causes harm.
Social Engineering
Manipulating people into revealing confidential information or granting access, often through deception or trust exploitation.

Strategies to prevent Cybercrime include:

Firewalls
A security barrier that filters incoming and outgoing traffic, blocking harmful data and preventing unauthorised access to the network.
VPNs
A virtual private network, or VPN, is an encrypted connection over the internet from a device to a network. The encrypted connection helps ensure that sensitive data is safely transmitted. It prevents unauthorized people from eavesdropping on the traffic and allows the user to conduct work remotely.
Encryption
Scrambling data so only authorised users with the correct key can read it.
2FA
Two- factor authentication requires two proofs of identity, such as a password plus a code or biometric.
