What is a Topology? Topologies are often represented as a graph. Network topologies describe the arrangement of networks and the relative location of traffic flows.

Glossary

LANs vs WANs
LAN: Series of computers connected to the same, primary ethernet cable. WAN: Large‑area network connecting cities or countries. Slower due to distance, often managed by multiple organisations or service providers.
Client-Server Model
A network where clients request services and a central server provides resources like files, security, and applications.
Bus Topology
All devices share a single backbone cable; cheap but collisions increase as more devices join.
Star Topology
All devices connect to a central hub or switch; reliable and easy to manage but depends on the central device.
Ring Topology
Devices form a circle and data travels in one direction; performance is predictable but one failure breaks the network.
Mesh Topology
Every device connects to multiple others, providing high redundancy and reliability but is expensive to install.
Hybrid Topology
A combination of two or more topologies (e.g., star‑bus), used to meet specific performance or layout needs.
Navigation Maps
A navigation map defines the structured path, architecture, or graphical layout connecting different screens, pages, or functional nodes in a software application or website.
