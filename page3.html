What are transmission media? Transmission media are the physical or wireless pathways that carry data signals between devices in a network.

Wired Transmission

Sending data through physical cables such as copper or fibre, offering high speed and low interference.

Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) Cable
Unshielded Twisted Pair cable used in Ethernet networks; cheap, flexible, and suitable for most LAN connections.
Copper Wire
Traditional electrical conductor used for data transmission; inexpensive but suffers from interference and signal loss over distance.
Coaxial Cable
Cable with a central conductor and shielding, offering better noise protection than UTP and used for broadband connections.
Optical Fibre
Uses pulses of light through glass fibres to transmit data at extremely high speeds over long distances with minimal loss.

Wireless Transmission

Transmitted through the air using electromagnetic energy. Most use radio-wave frequencies within the electromagnetic spectrum. Different frequencies are used for different purposes depending on the required distance, speed and reliability of the transmission.

Wi-Fi
Wi-Fi is what allows you to get access to high speed internet access on many devices without the need for a wired network. Wi-Fi technology uses radio waves to transmit data, operating mainly on the 2.4GHz and 5GHz frequency band.
Cellular Network
Mobile phone networks that use radio waves to send data. The signal needs to be received from a mobile phone tower which is then connected to the vast cellular network composed of wired and wireless connections.
Satellite
Satellite communication relies on a network of satellites in orbit around the earth, which receives and transmits data signals on radio waves. The satellite dish is equipped with a transmitter and receiver which communicates with the satellites in orbit. This however adds a noticeable delay in signal transmission due to the distance.
Bluetooth
Bluetooth is the exchange between devices such as phones and headphones. It is power efficient which reduces the battery requirements for wireless connection between devices around 10 metres apart.
Zigbee, Z-Wave and Matter
Low-power wireless communication protocols used for IoT devices, enabling smart home automation with short-range connectivity.
